## 2 Oriented Apparent Contours

Take a compact surface \(S\), connected and without boundary. If \(f: S \rightarrow \mathrm{RP}^{2}\) is a generic mapping, its critical point set is the union of \(s\) circles \(C_{1}, \ldots, C_{\mathrm{s}}\) embedded in \(S\). They constitute the fold curve of \(f\) and their images are the irreducible components of the apparent contour \(\gamma\), which is a curve with cusps and double points [W2, Ma]. We are going to see that \(f\) determines, in a "canonical" way, two distinct primitives for this curve. They belong to different equivalence classes, hence their sum must be equal to a constant. We will realize that this constant has a topological meaning, being equal to the Euler-Poincaré characteristic \(\chi(S)\) of the surface.
