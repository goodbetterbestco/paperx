[Generated abstract from paper content.] This paper studies how visibility and occlusion vary over the space of viewpoints for a polyhedral object and formalizes two complementary structures: the viewpoint space partition and its dual, the aspect graph. It characterizes the visual events that change aspect, derives worst-case complexity bounds for convex and non-convex polyhedra under orthographic and perspective viewing, and develops algorithms for constructing the associated partitions and graphs. The analysis shows that convex cases are comparatively tractable, whereas non-convex cases can generate extremely large numbers of distinct views, limiting the direct practicality of full aspect graphs.
